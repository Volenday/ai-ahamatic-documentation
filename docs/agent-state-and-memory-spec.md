# Agent State and Memory Spec — AI ahaMatic

This document defines **the state the agent may hold, carry, and hand off as it works, and the discipline that keeps that state trustworthy, safe to resume from, and free of what must never be retained** — what working state may and may not be persisted across the steps of a task and across sessions, what must hold of persisted state's integrity and provenance for it to be trusted, what a resume or a handoff requires to be safe and reproducible, and the absolute prohibition on retaining a secret or sensitive data in memory. It states **what** the agent may persist and what must be true of it; it does not describe how any state is stored, checkpointed, serialized, transferred, or tooled.

This is an Execution-phase artifact — the sixth document of the Meta-Operations domain. It inherits its framing from the Vision and Charter and is subordinate to it; where it appears to conflict with the charter, the charter prevails. It operates entirely within the authority model of `agent-operating-charter.md`: persisting state, resuming from it, and handing it off are each autonomous actions subject to that document's preconditions (§5), its permitted-action classes (§6), its approval boundary (§7), and its duty to halt and escalate (§8), and every conflict this document meets is resolved by that document's canonical precedence order (§4), which this document consumes and applies but never redefines. It holds every invariant of `system-invariants.md` §4 continuously — no state the agent persists or carries ever holds a secret (INV-03), draws one tenant's state into an action for another (INV-01), admits domain content into the core (INV-05, INV-06), or moves data across a region boundary in violation of residency (INV-07). It makes the attributable, logged, and reconstructable record of `audit-and-traceability.md` §3, its agent-action logging of §6, and its minimum traceability for autonomous change of §7 the substrate on which state provenance rests and the authoritative account against which carried state is reconciled. It treats the personal- and sensitive-data classification, retention, minimization, and redaction rules of `data-governance-and-privacy.md` and the absolute secrets rule of `security-policy.md` §4 (INV-03) as the standard for what may never be retained, the required-context and missing-or-stale-context rules of `prompt-and-context-management.md` §5 and §8 as the discipline any carried state re-enters an action under, the per-task and per-session envelopes of `token-and-compute-budget.md` §5 as the bound persistence draws against, the maximum iterations and retries of `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5 as the count a resume accrues against, and the approval and escalation mechanics and default-to-halt of `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` as the machinery a handoff on escalation and a halt on unsafe state hand to — citing each, redefining none. Every rule here is **platform-level and domain-neutral** — it holds for any component, any tenant, any region, and any lifecycle phase, regardless of what any builder builds with the platform.

This document owns what the governing documents deferred to it for managing the agent's own working state, memory, and handoffs reliably and reproducibly: **the rules for what may and may not be persisted across steps and sessions**, **the state-integrity and provenance rules that make persisted state trustworthy**, **the safe resume and handoff requirements**, and **the prohibition on retaining sensitive data in memory**. It does not own the invariants themselves, the preconditions and precedence order and approval boundary (owned by `agent-operating-charter.md`), the mandatory audit events, immutability, agent-action logging, and minimum traceability on which provenance rests (`audit-and-traceability.md`), the personal- and sensitive-data categories, retention and deletion, consent and minimization, and redaction rules (`data-governance-and-privacy.md`), the absolute secrets-handling rule (`security-policy.md` §4), the referential and migration-safety rules for committed data behind INV-04 (`data-model-and-entity-spec.md`), the required context per task type and the missing-or-stale-context prohibition (`prompt-and-context-management.md`), the token, compute, and cost envelopes (`token-and-compute-budget.md`), the maximum iterations and retries (`agent-loop-constraints.md`), the concrete numeric retention and resource values (`non-functional-requirements.md`), how any halt, approval, or escalation is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved (`human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`), or the ordered fallback ladder that governs recovery from a detected unsafe state (`self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md`) — each remains owned by the document already assigned to it, cited throughout, never redefined here.

## 1. Purpose and Reading Order

The document answers four questions:

- **What working state, memory, persistence, provenance, and a handoff are**, and how a prohibited retention, a corrupted or unattributable state, and an unsafe resume differ from one another and from an invariant, a precondition, a budget, and stale context.
- **What state may and may not be persisted across the steps of a task and across sessions**, enumerated and keyed to the document that owns each bound, so that nothing prohibited is ever carried and nothing a task needs to resume safely is dropped.
- **What must be true of persisted state's integrity and provenance for it to be trusted**, and what a resume or a handoff requires to be safe and reproducible.
- **What may never be retained in memory** — a secret or sensitive data — absolutely and without waiver.

It is structured as a pyramid: first the concepts of working state and memory and the failure modes they guard against, then the preconditions every persistence, resume, and handoff inherits, then what may and may not be persisted, then the integrity and provenance that make persisted state trustworthy, then the safe resume and handoff requirements built on it, then the prohibition on retaining sensitive data that gives the whole its force.

## 2. Scope of This Model

Working state and memory are properties of the platform's own autonomous agent, not of any one thing built on it. The model applies along three dimensions at once.

- **Bound to the agent's platform-layer state.** This document governs the state the agent itself holds, persists, resumes, and hands off at the platform layer — the working state by which it reasons, evaluates, generates, applies, corrects, and recovers, and the memory it carries across the steps of a task and across sessions. What state, memory, or persistence a builder configures inside their own built artifact — what a built application stores, checkpoints, or carries for its own behavior — is builder-defined content and is never absorbed into this document; the builder / built separation holds for state and memory exactly as it holds for every other primitive. The rules here bound the agent's own state alone, never satisfied or excused by the state handling of any single built application.
- **Across every lifecycle phase.** The rules bind continuously — Strategy, Guardrails, Design, Execution, and Evolution alike — exactly as the invariants the agent never breaches do (`system-invariants.md` §5). No phase relaxes the requirement; state persisted during a decision, a generated change, a promotion, an observation, a correction, and a recovery is each subject to the same rules.
- **Across every tenant and every region.** Every persistence rule, integrity and provenance requirement, and resume and handoff obligation holds identically for each tenant and in each operating region; no state is retained, resumed, or handed off in a way that widens for one tenant's or one region's convenience. Consistent with tenant isolation (INV-01) and residency (INV-07), the agent never persists or carries one tenant's state into an action for another, and never persists or transfers state across a region boundary in violation of residency.

The model is domain-neutral: every persistence rule, integrity and provenance requirement, and resume and handoff obligation is expressed in terms that hold regardless of what is built on the platform, and none presumes the characteristics of any single domain.

## 3. What Working State, Memory, and a Handoff Are

The core objective of this document rests on a small set of concepts that must not be conflated.

- **Working state** is the transient state the agent holds within a single step to reason and act — the in-flight state of the action it is currently performing, not carried beyond it.
- **Memory** is state the agent deliberately carries beyond the immediate action — across the steps of a task or across sessions — so that later work can continue from it.
- **Persistence** is the act of carrying state across a step or session boundary into memory. State is persisted, never assumed to persist: only what a rule of §5 permits to be carried is carried, and only for as long as its purpose requires.
- **Provenance** is the record of where a piece of state came from, when, and under whose authority and trigger — what makes carried state attributable and therefore trustworthy. State without established provenance is treated as unattributed, never as trusted-by-default.
- **State integrity** is the property that persisted state remains valid, consistent, and uncorrupted between the point it is written and the point it is used, and faithfully reflects what the reconstructable record attests happened. State that cannot be shown intact is treated as corrupt.
- **A resume** is the continuation of a task from a persisted checkpoint after a pause, interruption, or session boundary.
- **A handoff** is the transfer of the state of work from one execution context, session, or process to another — or to a human on escalation — so that the work can be continued.
- **Reproducibility** is the property that a resume or handoff from a given persisted state yields a continuation consistent with what the trace establishes — not a divergent one dependent on unrecorded state. It is what makes state management reliable rather than merely possible.

This document guards against three distinct failure modes, each of which the rules below are shaped to prevent:

| Failure Mode                      | What It Is                                                                                                                                                                                                     | Primarily Guarded By                                                                    |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Prohibited retention              | Persisting state that must never be held — a secret, personal or sensitive data beyond its authorized handling, one tenant's state carried into another's action, or state whose retention violates residency. | The persistence rules (§5); the prohibition on retaining sensitive data in memory (§8). |
| Corrupted or unattributable state | Carrying forward state whose integrity or provenance cannot be established — acting on state that may be corrupt, or that cannot be attributed and reconciled against the record.                              | The state-integrity and provenance rules (§6).                                          |

It requires the right state be held safely; it never redefines what that state means.
- **State carried forward is only as good as its provenance and currency, and re-enters a later action as context.** Persisted state that a later step resumes from, or that a handoff transfers, becomes an input to the context that step assembles (`prompt-and-context-management.md` §5); state whose currency or integrity cannot be established is stale or corrupt, and acting on it is forbidden exactly as acting on stale context is (that document's §8). This document keeps carried state trustworthy at the boundary so the context discipline can rely on it; it never becomes a path to smuggle stale or prohibited state past that discipline.

## 4. Preconditions Bind Every Persistence, Resume, and Handoff

Because persisting state, resuming from it, and handing it off are each autonomous actions, every precondition of `agent-operating-charter.md` §5 binds them. Holding state confers no authority the underlying action would otherwise lack, and no volume of retained memory is a licence to act outside those preconditions.

- **Each is within granted authority and in scope.** Persisting, resuming, and handing off state falls within the agent's granted authority and the authorized scope of `vision-and-charter.md` §3, admits no domain content into the core (INV-05, INV-06), and is never used to carry forward a non-goal of its §4; memory never becomes a store through which the platform absorbs builder-defined content.
- **Each holds every invariant.** Holding or transferring state breaches no invariant of `system-invariants.md` §4 — it never renders a secret into stored state (INV-03), never carries one tenant's state into an action for another (INV-01), and never persists or moves state across a region boundary in violation of residency (INV-07) in the act of persisting, resuming, or handing off.
- **Each draws against the resource envelope.** Persisting and resuming state consumes token, compute, and cost against the per-task and per-session envelopes of `token-and-compute-budget.md` §5 and accrues against the iteration count of `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5; memory is not an unbounded store, and a resume is not a device to spend beyond, or reset, a bound the task already carries.
- **Each leaves its trace.** What state was persisted, resumed, or handed off is part of the minimum traceability of `audit-and-traceability.md` §7 and its agent-action record (§6); a persistence, resume, or handoff that cannot be attributed and reconstructed is itself a traceability violation, escalated under `agent-operating-charter.md` §5.
- **One that cannot clear a precondition does not proceed.** Where any precondition above cannot be established, the state is not persisted, the task is not resumed, and the work is not handed off autonomously; it is halted and escalated by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`, consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3.

## 5. What May and May Not Be Persisted Across Steps and Sessions

State is persisted only where carrying it is necessary to continue or hand off the task, is authorized, and is free of prohibited content; state is not persisted merely because it is available or convenient. The requirement is deny-by-default and minimizing: the agent persists the minimum a safe, reproducible continuation requires, and nothing more. This document enumerates the disposition of each class of state; what makes a given datum a secret, personal, sensitive, or referentially valid is owned by the document named, which alone defines its content.

| State Class                                                                                                                         | Disposition                                                                                                                                 | Bound By                                                       |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Task progress toward its termination state — sub-goals resolved, uncertainties narrowed, the reconstructable checkpoint of the work | May be persisted, as the minimum a safe resume or handoff requires                                                                          | `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5; `audit-and-traceability.md` §7 |
| Decisions and evaluations the agent has made and their outcomes                                                                     | May be persisted, carried with the trace they already produce                                                                               | `audit-and-traceability.md` §6, §7                             |
| Provenance and attribution references for carried state                                                                             | Must accompany any persisted state                                                                                                          | `audit-and-traceability.md` §6, §7; §6 of this document        |
| An authorization a resumed action rests on                                                                                          | May be carried as part of the trace, and re-cleared on resume; never reused for a different or broadened action                             | `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` §7                             |
| Assembled governing context — invariants, floors, rules a task conformed to                                                         | Re-assembled and re-verified at the point of use, never persisted as authoritative; a carried copy is treated as stale until re-established | `prompt-and-context-management.md` §5, §8                      |
| Secrets — credentials, keys, tokens                                                                                                 | Never persisted, in any form, in any stored state                                                                                           | INV-03; `security-policy.md` §4; §8 of this document           |
| Personal or sensitive data beyond its authorized handling                                                                           | Never persisted; redacted or withheld at the point it would be captured                                                                     | `data-governance-and-privacy.md` §4–§8; §8 of this document    |
| One tenant's state, for use in an action for another                                                                                | Never persisted or carried across the tenant boundary                                                                                       | INV-01                                                         |
| Residency-bound state, across a region boundary                                                                                     | Never persisted or transferred in violation of residency                                                                                    | INV-07                                                         |
| Domain content, terminology, or workflow                                                                                            | Never persisted into the core; would couple a primitive to a built artifact                                                                 | INV-05, INV-06                                                 |

- **Persisted state is bounded by purpose, not convenience.** State is carried only for as long as its purpose requires and is subject to the same retention and deletion obligations as any held data (`data-governance-and-privacy.md` §5); memory is never a store that outlives the task or session whose continuation it served, and persistence is never a path to hold data past a retention basis that has ended.
- **The minimum needed to resume is a floor; minimization is the ceiling.** A task persists everything a safe, reproducible continuation requires and nothing it does not: dropping state a safe resume needs makes the resume unsafe (§7), and carrying state beyond that need is a minimization violation (`data-governance-and-privacy.md` §6). Neither is traded for the other.
- **What is prohibited is prohibited absolutely.** No purpose, urgency, or convenience of continuation permits persisting a secret, sensitive data beyond its authorization, cross-tenant state, or residency-violating state; the prohibitions above are never relaxed to make a resume or handoff easier (§8).

## 6. State-Integrity and Provenance Rules

Persisted state is safe to act on only if it can be trusted — established as intact, attributable to where it came from, and reconcilable with what actually happened. This document owns the requirement that carried state have integrity and provenance; it does not own the audit record on which provenance rests, or the referential rules that govern committed data.

- **Every piece of carried state carries its provenance.** Persisted state is attributable — to the specific agent process, triggering task, and authority under which it was produced (`audit-and-traceability.md` §6) — and is never carried forward as an undifferentiated fact detached from its origin. State whose provenance cannot be established is unattributed and is not trusted or acted upon.
- **The reconstructable record is authoritative, not the agent's memory.** Carried state is trusted only insofar as it can be reconciled against the attributable, logged, and reconstructable record (`audit-and-traceability.md` §3, §7); where memory and the record diverge, the record governs. The agent never treats its own recollection as the source of truth in place of the record, consistent with that document's requirement that what happened be reconstructable without recourse to an actor's own account.
- **Persisted state remains intact between write and use.** State that cannot be shown to be valid, consistent, and uncorrupted from the point it was written to the point it is used is treated as corrupt and is not acted upon. Where the agent's state persists into committed platform data, its referential validity is governed by INV-04, whose rules are owned by `data-model-and-entity-spec.md`; this document requires that integrity be established and redefines none of those rules.
- **A change to persisted state is a traced transition, and the record of it is immutable.** Updating carried state is itself a consequential action producing the agent-action record of `audit-and-traceability.md` §6; a superseded state is corrected by an appended transition that references what it replaces, never by silently rewriting the record of what the state was (that document's §5). Integrity rests on the immutable trail of transitions, not on mutable memory.
- **Stale state is not integral for the purpose of acting.** State that no longer reflects the current reconstructable record or the current governing rules is stale; it is treated as stale context and is not acted upon (`prompt-and-context-management.md` §8). Carried state includes what is needed to establish whether it is still current.
- **Uncertainty about integrity, provenance, or currency resolves against trusting the state.** Where it cannot be established that carried state is intact, attributable, and current, it is re-derived or re-verified before use, or the action halts; the agent never proceeds on a favorable assumption about state it cannot establish, consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3. A detected corruption or a provenance gap in state a task must rely on is an unsafe condition, recovered from along the ordered fallback ladder of `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md` or escalated, never worked around.

## 7. Safe Resume and Handoff Requirements

The purpose of persisted state is to be resumed from or handed off; a resume or handoff is safe only if it continues from state that is intact, attributable, current, and free of prohibited content, and only if the continuation is reproducible. This document owns what a safe resume and handoff require; the mechanics of an escalation handoff and of any halt are owned by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`.

- **A resume proceeds only from a trustworthy checkpoint.** The agent resumes a task only from a checkpoint established as intact and attributable (§6) and reachable as a reversible, reconstructable state (INV-08); it never resumes from a partial, corrupt, or unverified state. A checkpoint that cannot be shown trustworthy is not a resumable state.
- **A resumed action re-clears every precondition and re-verifies its context.** On resume, the action re-establishes every precondition of `agent-operating-charter.md` §5 and re-assembles and re-verifies the context its type requires (`prompt-and-context-management.md` §5) before continuing; it inherits neither authority nor currency from the paused state. A prior approval a gated action rested on is not reused by a resume — the resumed action re-clears it exactly as any authorized action must (`human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` §7).
- **Stale state on resume is treated as missing.** State that can no longer be shown current at the moment of resume is stale; the task does not continue on it, and does not proceed on a remembered or assumed version — it re-derives what it needs or halts (`prompt-and-context-management.md` §8).
- **A handoff carries what is needed to continue, and nothing prohibited.** A handoff transfers the reconstructable state of the work, its provenance, its progress toward termination, and any authorization it rests on — while never carrying a secret (INV-03; §8), personal or sensitive data beyond its authorized handling (`data-governance-and-privacy.md`; §8), one tenant's state across the boundary (INV-01), or residency-violating state (INV-07). A handoff that could complete only by carrying prohibited state does not proceed.
- **A handoff on escalation hands to the human protocol.** Where a handoff is an escalation, how it is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved is owned by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`; this document requires only that the handed-off state be complete, intact, attributed, and free of prohibited content, recorded to the standard `audit-and-traceability.md` owns. This document establishes what a safe handoff requires; that document carries the escalation out.
- **Resume and handoff are reproducible.** A continuation from a given checkpoint produces a result consistent with what the trace establishes, not one dependent on unrecorded state; state whose absence from the record would make a continuation non-reproducible is not relied upon. Reproducibility is what makes a resume or handoff reliable rather than merely possible.
- **Resume and handoff stay within the bounds the task already carries.** A resume draws against the same resource envelope and iteration ceiling the task began under (`token-and-compute-budget.md` §5; `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5); a task is never paused and resumed, or split across sessions, to evade a ceiling, a budget, or an approval it would otherwise reach. Every resume and handoff is itself a consequential action producing the audit record `audit-and-traceability.md` §4 requires.

## 8. Prohibition on Retaining Sensitive Data in Memory

The prohibition on retaining sensitive data in memory is the obligation that gives every rule above its force. It resolves every uncertainty about what may be held in one direction: toward not retaining. A secret is never retained, and personal or sensitive data is never retained in memory beyond what its authorized handling permits — absolutely, in any form of stored state, across any step or session boundary.

- **A secret is never retained.** No credential, key, token, or other secret is ever persisted, carried across a step or session, or held in any stored state, extending the absolute no-secret-exposure rule of `security-policy.md` §4 (INV-03) to the agent's own memory. This holds regardless of how convenient retaining it would make a resume or handoff; a secret required for a later step is re-supplied through the means that document owns, never remembered.
- **Personal and sensitive data are retained only within their authorized handling.** Personal or sensitive data is never held in memory beyond what its classification tier, retention basis, and minimization obligation permit (`data-governance-and-privacy.md` §4–§6), and is redacted or withheld from any persisted state not authorized to hold it (that document's §8). Memory is not an exemption from those obligations; carrying such data past its basis is itself a retention and minimization violation.
- **Cross-tenant and residency-bound state are never retained across their boundary.** Memory never holds one tenant's state for use in an action for another (INV-01), and never persists or carries residency-bound state across a region boundary in violation of residency (INV-07); no continuation convenience licenses either.
- **The prohibition is never resolved by proceeding.** Prohibited state is never retained by assuming it is safe, by holding a partial or transformed version whose sensitivity cannot be ruled out, or by carrying it "only briefly." Where it cannot be established that persisted state is free of a secret or of personal or sensitive data beyond its authorization, it is treated as carrying that data and is not persisted — redacted or withheld accordingly, matching the deny-by-default posture of `data-governance-and-privacy.md` §7 and §8 and `security-policy.md` §4.
- **A prohibited retention is a blocking violation.** Retention discovered in memory is not a degradation to be measured; it stops and is escalated. Where it coincides with a breach of an invariant, it additionally halts and escalates under `system-invariants.md` §3, and an exposure of personal or sensitive data is classified and blocks under `data-governance-and-privacy.md` §7 without this document redefining that rule. A halt on prohibited retention produces the audit record `audit-and-traceability.md` §4 requires — including what was found and the state of the work when it was halted — while the record itself never captures the prohibited data (that document's §5).
- **The prohibition is non-negotiable and always-on.** It binds every lifecycle phase and every tenant and region, and is never suspended for urgency, for the convenience of a resume or handoff, for apparent proximity to completion, or for a low-apparent-risk continuation; no level of authority waives it.

## 9. Precedence and Ownership Boundaries

When a rule in this document meets any other consideration, it is resolved by the precedence order of `agent-operating-charter.md` §4, at whose apex the Vision and Charter sits.

- **The charter prevails.** No rule in this document is applied in a way that contradicts the Vision and Charter; where this document appears to conflict with the charter, the charter governs.
- **This document grants no authority a higher layer withholds.** The rules here only require state to be safe to hold, resume from, and hand off; they never extend autonomy past a bound another document sets, and never confer authority the invariants or the guardrail, architecture, and delivery layers withhold.
- **A guarantee is never spent to persist, resume, or hand off state.** No invariant, floor, or threshold is relaxed to carry state forward, to make a resume succeed, or to complete a handoff; state retained or continued at the cost of a guarantee is never a net gain.

This document owns the rules for what may and may not be persisted across steps and sessions, the state-integrity and provenance rules, the safe resume and handoff requirements, and the prohibition on retaining sensitive data in memory. It does not own the specifics other documents govern, and none of those documents may weaken this model:

- **The invariants themselves** — their binary form, the blocking-check rule, and the halt-and-escalate rule — are owned by `system-invariants.md`; this document holds all persisted, resumed, and handed-off state to them and redefines none.
- **The preconditions for autonomous action, the document-precedence order, the permitted and approval-required action classes, and the duty to halt and escalate** are owned by `agent-operating-charter.md`; this document binds every persistence, resume, and handoff to those preconditions and applies that precedence order to resolve a conflict, without restating either.
- **The mandatory audit events, immutability and tamper-evidence, agent-action logging, and the minimum traceability for autonomous change** are owned by `audit-and-traceability.md`; this document rests provenance on that record and reconciles carried state against it, without restating its elements.
- **The personal- and sensitive-data categories, the classification-and-handling matrix, retention and deletion, consent and minimization, PII-exposure as a blocking violation, and redaction** are owned by `data-governance-and-privacy.md`; this document forbids retaining such data in memory beyond that authorization without redefining any of it.
- **The absolute no-secret-exposure rule and secrets handling** are owned by `security-policy.md` §4 (INV-03); this document extends it to the agent's memory without relaxing it, and its means of re-supplying secrets in place of retention are owned by `environment-and-config-spec.md`.
- **The referential and migration-safety rules for committed data** behind INV-04 are owned by `data-model-and-entity-spec.md`; this document requires integrity of state that persists into committed data without restating those rules.
- **The required context per task type, the context-window and scoping limits, and the prohibition on acting with missing or stale context** are owned by `prompt-and-context-management.md`; this document keeps carried state trustworthy and current so that a resume does not act on stale context, without redefining that discipline.
- **The maximum iterations and retries** are owned by `agent-loop-constraints.md`; this document accrues a resume against that count and never treats a resume as a way to reset it.
- **The token, compute, and cost envelopes** are owned by `token-and-compute-budget.md`; this document draws persistence and resume against that envelope without redefining it.
- **The concrete numeric retention and resource values** are owned by `non-functional-requirements.md`; this document requires state be bounded and retained only within its basis, and keys to those values without minting a number.
- **How a halt, an approval, or an escalation is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved, and the default to halt when a trigger is ambiguous** are owned by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`; this document establishes what a safe handoff and a halt on unsafe state require, not the mechanics that carry them out.
- **The ordered fallback ladder and its maximum attempts** for recovering from a detected corrupt or unsafe state are owned by `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md`; this document treats a detected integrity or resume failure as a condition that ladder governs, without defining it.
- **What state or memory a builder configures inside their own built artifact** is builder-defined content and is never governed by this document; this document binds the agent's own platform-layer state alone.

## 10. Binding Rules

These rules hold for every action the agent takes subject to this model and are subordinate to the charter.

- **Only necessary, permitted state is persisted.** State is carried across a step or session only where a safe, reproducible continuation requires it and no rule forbids it; nothing prohibited is ever persisted, and nothing a safe resume needs is dropped.
- **Every persistence, resume, and handoff clears the preconditions of autonomy.** Each is granted, in scope, invariant-holding, reversible, and traceable, and draws against the resource envelope; one that cannot satisfy a precondition does not proceed and escalates.
- **Persisted state is intact and attributable, or it is not trusted.** Carried state has established integrity and provenance and is reconciled against the reconstructable record; state that cannot be shown intact, attributed, and current is re-derived, re-verified, or not acted upon.
- **A resume re-clears preconditions and re-verifies currency.** A resumed action re-establishes every precondition and re-assembles its required context; stale state on resume is treated as missing, and a prior approval is never reused by a resume.
- **A handoff carries what is needed and nothing prohibited.** It transfers the reconstructable state, its provenance, and any authorization it rests on, free of secrets, sensitive data beyond authorization, cross-tenant state, and residency-violating state; an escalation handoff hands to the human protocol.
- **Retaining a secret or sensitive data in memory is prohibited absolutely.** No secret is ever retained, and personal or sensitive data is never held beyond its authorized handling; uncertainty about whether persisted state carries prohibited data resolves toward not retaining it.
- **The prohibition is non-negotiable.** No urgency, continuation convenience, proximity to completion, or authority waives it; a prohibited retention is a blocking violation, not a degradation.
- **The reconstructable record, not the agent's memory, is authoritative.** Where carried state and the record diverge, the record governs; memory never substitutes for it as the source of truth.
- **What state was persisted, resumed, or handed off is traceable and attributable.** Every persistence, resume, handoff, and halt on unsafe or prohibited state produces the record `audit-and-traceability.md` requires, without the record itself capturing prohibited data.
- **The content classifications, numeric ceilings, and escalation mechanics are owned elsewhere.** This document requires that state be safe to hold, resume from, and hand off; what makes a datum sensitive or a secret, the numeric retention and resource values, the context discipline, and the escalation mechanics are owned by the documents assigned to them.
- **The builder / built separation holds throughout.** This document binds the agent's own platform-layer state and memory; the state a builder configures inside their own built artifact remains the builder's own.
- **Everything remains domain-neutral and platform-level.** No persistence rule, integrity or provenance requirement, or resume, handoff, or retention rule in this document encodes the characteristics of any single domain; all remain valid for the platform, in any tenant and any region.
