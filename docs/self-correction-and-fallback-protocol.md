# Self-Correction and Fallback Protocol — AI ahaMatic

This document defines **how the agent detects an error or a regression in its own conduct and recovers from it predictably, along an ordered fallback ladder, without compounding harm** — the signals by which an error or regression in the agent's own work is detected and that trigger a correction, the ordered ladder of recovery the agent follows (retry, then a safe alternative, then a rollback, then human escalation), the finite maximum number of self-correction attempts it may make before escalation is mandatory, and the conditions under which no further autonomous action may be taken at all. It states **what** a self-correction requires and when it must stop; it does not describe how any error is detected, or how any correction, retry, rollback, or escalation is orchestrated, instrumented, or tooled.

This is an Evolution-phase artifact — the seventh document of the Meta-Operations domain, and its second Evolution-phase artifact, distinct from the five Guardrails-phase siblings and the one Execution-phase sibling. It inherits its framing from the Vision and Charter and is subordinate to it; where it appears to conflict with the charter, the charter prevails. It operates entirely within the authority model of `agent-operating-charter.md`: every correction attempt is an autonomous action subject to that document's preconditions (§5), its permitted-action classes (§6), its approval boundary (§7), and its duty to halt and escalate (§8), and every conflict this document meets is resolved by that document's canonical precedence order (§4). It holds every invariant of `system-invariants.md` §4 continuously — a correction that would produce or sustain a breach halts under that document's §3 and never advances to a further rung — and it makes the minimum traceability of `audit-and-traceability.md` §7 and the agent-action logging of its §6, under which every attempt along the ladder is recorded, a per-attempt obligation. It treats the health signals that may trigger self-correction or a rollback of `observability-and-monitoring.md` §7 and the escalation-mandating signals and runtime severity of its §8 and §5 as the inputs that trigger the ladder; the mandatory rollback path, triggers, and time-to-recover target of `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §5–§7 as the rung the ladder invokes to reverse a change; the containment-first ordering, mandatory escalation thresholds, and prohibited unilateral recovery actions of `incident-response-and-recovery.md` §5, §7, and §8 as the response the ladder hands off to on exhaustion; the maximum iterations and retries of `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5 and the per-task and per-session envelopes of `token-and-compute-budget.md` §5 as the outer bounds that bound the ladder from without; the state-integrity, provenance, and safe-resume requirements of `agent-state-and-memory-spec.md` §6 and §7 and the missing-or-stale-context prohibition of `prompt-and-context-management.md` §8 as conditions a correction may neither create nor act through; and the escalation mechanics and default-to-halt of `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` as the machinery the terminal rung hands to — citing each, redefining none. Every rule here is **platform-level and domain-neutral** — it holds for any component, any tenant, any region, and any lifecycle phase, regardless of what any builder builds with the platform.

This document owns what the governing documents deferred to it for detecting the agent's own errors and recovering predictably without compounding harm: **the error- and regression-detection signals that trigger a correction**, **the ordered fallback ladder — retry, then a safe alternative, then a rollback, then human escalation**, **the maximum self-correction attempts before mandatory escalation**, and **the conditions that forbid any further autonomous action**. It does not own the invariants themselves, the preconditions and precedence order and approval boundary (owned by `agent-operating-charter.md`), the mandatory audit events, immutability, and agent-action logging (`audit-and-traceability.md`), the health and escalation-mandating signals it responds to (`observability-and-monitoring.md`), the rollback path, triggers, and recovery target (`release-and-rollback-protocol.md`), the incident classification, containment-first ordering, backup and restore guarantees, and prohibited unilateral recovery (`incident-response-and-recovery.md`), the maximum iterations and retries (`agent-loop-constraints.md`), the token, compute, and cost envelopes (`token-and-compute-budget.md`), the state-integrity, provenance, and safe-resume requirements (`agent-state-and-memory-spec.md`), the required context and the missing-or-stale-context prohibition (`prompt-and-context-management.md`), the concrete numeric values (`non-functional-requirements.md`), or how any halt, approval, or escalation is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved (`human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`) — each remains owned by the document already assigned to it, cited throughout, never redefined here.

## 1. Purpose and Reading Order

The document answers four questions:

- **What an error, a regression, a self-correction, and the fallback ladder are**, and how an undetected error, a correction that compounds harm, and a correction without end differ from one another and from an invariant, an iteration, a budget, a rollback, and an incident.
- **What signals detect an error or a regression in the agent's own work and trigger a correction**, so that the agent recognizes its own error rather than proceeding on it.
- **What ordered ladder the recovery follows, and how many self-correction attempts the agent may make**, before escalation is mandatory.
- **What conditions forbid any further autonomous action** — where the ladder does not proceed and the agent halts and escalates, absolutely and without waiver.

It is structured as a pyramid: first the concepts of an error and its recovery and the failure modes they guard against, then the preconditions every correction attempt inherits, then the signals that detect an error and trigger the ladder, then the ordered ladder itself, then the maximum attempts that bound it, then the conditions that forbid any further autonomous action and give the whole its force.

## 2. Scope of This Model

Self-correction and fallback are properties of the platform's own autonomous agent, not of any one thing built on it. The model applies along three dimensions at once.

- **Bound to the agent's platform-layer self-correction.** This document governs how the agent recovers from an error or regression in its own work at the platform layer — the recovery by which it corrects a change, an evaluation, or an action it produced or took. What self-correction, retry, or fallback logic a builder configures inside their own built artifact — how a built application detects and recovers from its own faults — is builder-defined content and is never absorbed into this document; the builder / built separation holds for self-correction exactly as it holds for every other primitive. The ladder here bounds the agent's own recovery alone, never satisfied or excused by the error handling of any single built application.
- **Across every lifecycle phase.** The protocol binds continuously — Evolution primarily, but a self-correction is not relaxed because the error it recovers from arose in an earlier phase. An error in a decision, a generated change, a promotion, an observation, a correction, and a recovery is each subject to the same ladder and the same bounds, exactly as the invariants the agent never breaches are (`system-invariants.md` §5).
- **Across every tenant and every region.** Every detection rule, ladder rung, attempt ceiling, and forbidding condition holds identically for each tenant and in each operating region; no recovery is weakened, skipped, or narrowed for one tenant's or one region's convenience. Consistent with tenant isolation (INV-01) and residency (INV-07), a correction affecting one tenant never observes, affects, or discloses another's state, and never moves data across a region boundary in violation of residency.

The model is domain-neutral: every detection rule, ladder rung, attempt ceiling, and forbidding condition is expressed in terms that hold regardless of what is built on the platform, and none presumes the characteristics of any single domain.

## 3. What an Error, a Regression, and the Fallback Ladder Are

The core objective of this document rests on a small set of concepts that must not be conflated.

- **An error** is a defect in the agent's own work — a change, evaluation, or action it produced or took that fails to hold a guarantee it must, fails to achieve its intended in-scope outcome, or leaves an invalid or unsafe state.
- **A regression** is a measured degradation of the running platform against a target it must hold, detected after release; its definition is owned by `non-functional-requirements.md` §11 and its runtime detection by `observability-and-monitoring.md` §5, and this document treats it as one class of signal that may trigger a correction.
- **Detection** is the recognition, from a signal owned elsewhere, that an error or regression has occurred; this document defines the response to a signal, never the signal.
- **A self-correction** is a bounded, in-place correction of the agent's own work short of reversing it — a retry or a safe alternative.
- **A rollback** is the reversal of a change to its last known-good state, owned by `release-and-rollback-protocol.md`, which the ladder invokes as a rung.
- **The fallback ladder** is the ordered sequence of recovery the agent follows on detecting an error or regression: retry, then a safe alternative, then a rollback, then human escalation.
- **A correction attempt** is a single autonomous step along the ladder short of the terminal escalation.
- **Compounding harm** is worsening the condition a recovery exists to resolve — spreading it, breaching a guarantee to reach recovery, or advancing into a state that cannot be safely left.
- **Escalation** is the terminal rung: handing the decision to a human once autonomous recovery is exhausted, unavailable, or forbidden.

This document guards against three distinct failure modes, each of which the rules below are shaped to prevent:

| Failure Mode           | What It Is                                                                                                                                                                         | Primarily Guarded By                                                                                  |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Undetected error       | Proceeding on an error or regression in the agent's own work without recognizing the signal that it has occurred.                                                                  | The error- and regression-detection signals (§5).                                                     |
| Compounding harm       | Recovering in a way that worsens the condition — breaching a guarantee to reach recovery, spreading the failure, applying a rung out of order, or entering an unrecoverable state. | The ordered fallback ladder (§6).                                                                     |
| Correction without end | Correcting past the point where escalation is mandatory, or acting autonomously where no further action is safe — retrying indefinitely rather than escalating.                    | The maximum self-correction attempts (§7); the conditions that forbid further autonomous action (§8). |

These are distinct from several related instruments, each owned elsewhere and never redefined here:

| ------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |

- **This document owns the response to a detected error; it never redefines the signal, the rollback, or the incident response.** The signals that detect an error or regression are owned by `observability-and-monitoring.md` and by the documents whose breach is itself a signal (`system-invariants.md`, `audit-and-traceability.md`, `agent-loop-constraints.md`, `token-and-compute-budget.md`, `agent-state-and-memory-spec.md`, `prompt-and-context-management.md`); the rollback the ladder invokes is owned by `release-and-rollback-protocol.md`; the containment-first recovery the ladder hands off to is owned by `incident-response-and-recovery.md`. This document defines the ordered response to those signals — which triggers a correction, in what order recovery proceeds, and when it must stop — and redefines none of them.

Every attempt is an iteration that counts against the task's ceiling (`agent-loop-constraints.md` §5) and a draw against the per-task and per-session envelope (`token-and-compute-budget.md` §5); those documents bound the ladder from the outside. The attempt ceiling of §7 is a distinct, nested bound this document owns; whichever bound — the attempt ceiling or an outer one — is reached first ends autonomous recovery and escalates.

## 4. Preconditions Bind Every Correction Attempt

Because each correction attempt is itself an autonomous action, every precondition of `agent-operating-charter.md` §5 binds every attempt, not merely the first. Recovering from an error confers no authority the underlying action would otherwise lack, and no error is a licence to act outside those preconditions.

- **Each attempt is within granted authority and in scope.** A correction falls within the agent's granted authority and the authorized scope of `vision-and-charter.md` §3, admits no domain content into the core (INV-05, INV-06), and never pursues a non-goal of its §4; recovering from an error is never an occasion to act outside scope.
- **Each attempt holds every invariant.** A correction is checked against the full invariant set of `system-invariants.md` §4 and breaches none; an attempt that would produce or sustain a breach does not proceed, and an invariant breach detected mid-ladder halts and escalates immediately under that document's §3 rather than advancing to a further rung.
- **Each attempt retains a reversible, recoverable path.** No correction advances the platform into a state it cannot safely leave (INV-08); a recovery that could only proceed irreversibly is not taken autonomously, and a correction that would leave a corrupt or unsafe-to-resume state (`agent-state-and-memory-spec.md` §6, §7) is not a recovery.
- **Each attempt leaves its trace.** Every attempt along the ladder establishes the minimum traceability of `audit-and-traceability.md` §7 and produces the agent-action record of its §6 — under which every attempt is recorded, not only the one that ultimately succeeds or the escalation that ends it. A correction that cannot be attributed and reconstructed is itself a traceability violation, escalated under `agent-operating-charter.md` §5.
- **Each attempt draws against the outer bounds.** A correction consumes an iteration against the ceiling of `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5 and token, compute, and cost against the envelope of `token-and-compute-budget.md` §5; recovery is not an unbounded activity, and no attempt is exempt from the counts a task already carries.
- **An attempt that cannot clear a precondition does not proceed.** Where any precondition above cannot be established, the correction is not taken autonomously; the agent halts and escalates (§8), consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3.

## 5. Error- and Regression-Detection Signals

The agent recognizes an error or a regression in its own work only through a signal, and every such signal is owned by another document. This document enumerates and classifies which signals trigger a correction and which forbid one; it defines none of them. Detection is deny-by-default: where it cannot be established that the agent's own work is free of error, the work is treated as in error and enters recovery, never proceeding on the favorable assumption.

| Detection Signal                                                                                             | Disposition                                                                                             | Owned By                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| A rollback-triggering health signal — a recoverable degradation of the running platform within bounds        | Triggers the fallback ladder (§6)                                                                       | `observability-and-monitoring.md` §7                                                  |
| A runtime regression against a non-functional target, detected after release                                 | Triggers the fallback ladder (§6) — correct in place or roll back                                       | `observability-and-monitoring.md` §5; `non-functional-requirements.md` §11            |
| An iteration that makes no progress, an oscillating loop, or a repeated no-op cycle                          | Triggers the fallback ladder (§6) where a changed condition permits; otherwise terminates and escalates | `agent-loop-constraints.md` §6, §7                                                    |
| A carried state that cannot be shown intact or attributable, or an unsafe resume condition                   | Triggers the fallback ladder (§6) or escalates; never worked around                                     | `agent-state-and-memory-spec.md` §6, §7                                               |
| Missing or stale context for the action being corrected                                                      | Re-derived or re-verified before any correction; a correction is never retried against stale context    | `prompt-and-context-management.md` §8                                                 |
| A runtime invariant breach, or a possible breach                                                             | Never a ladder input; immediate halt-and-escalate (§8)                                                  | `system-invariants.md` §3, §4                                                         |
| An escalation-mandating runtime signal, a monitoring blind spot, or an active Critical or High vulnerability | Never a ladder input; escalates at once (§8)                                                            | `observability-and-monitoring.md` §8                                                  |
| A reached iteration ceiling, exhausted budget, or exhausted attempt ceiling                                  | Never a ladder input; mandatory escalation (§8)                                                         | `agent-loop-constraints.md` §8; `token-and-compute-budget.md` §8; §7 of this document |

- **A signal owned elsewhere is the input; this document defines the response.** A detection signal is never redefined here, its threshold never restated or varied; this document classifies each signal as one that triggers a correction or one that forbids one, and orders the response.
- **The agent detects error in its own work, not only in the running platform.** A produced change that fails its own invariant-and-gate evaluation before it enters the system is refused at evaluation — the deny-by-default posture that is itself always within autonomy (`agent-operating-charter.md` §6) — and never applied and then corrected; a state that cannot be shown intact, a context that is stale, and an iteration that makes no progress are each detection signals to which recovery, not continuation, is the response.
- **A signal that mandates escalation is never routed into the ladder.** An invariant breach, an escalation-mandating runtime signal, a prohibited unilateral recovery condition, or an exhausted bound forbids a correction attempt and is handled under §8; it is never treated as a recoverable error the ladder may work through.
- **Uncertainty about whether the work is in error resolves toward recovery.** Where it cannot be established that the agent's own work holds every guarantee and achieved its intended in-scope outcome, it is treated as in error and enters the ladder or escalates, consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3.

## 6. The Ordered Fallback Ladder

On detecting a correctable error or regression (§5), the agent recovers along a fixed, ordered ladder: retry, then a safe alternative, then a rollback, then human escalation. The order is followed rung by rung — a rung is reached only where the ones before it are unavailable or insufficient — so that recovery is proportionate to the error and never compounds harm. This document owns the ladder and its ordering; it never redefines the rollback it invokes or the incident recovery it hands off to.

- **Rung 1 — Retry.** The agent re-attempts the failed action only where the condition that caused the failure has meaningfully changed; a retry against an unchanged condition is not progress and is not permitted (`agent-loop-constraints.md` §6). A retry is a bounded self-correction: it counts against the iteration ceiling (`agent-loop-constraints.md` §5), draws against the envelope (`token-and-compute-budget.md` §5), and counts against the attempt ceiling (§7).
- **Rung 2 — Safe alternative.** Where a retry is unavailable or insufficient, the agent may take a different in-bounds path to the intended outcome — one that clears every precondition of §4 and holds every invariant and floor. An alternative that could reach the outcome only by relaxing a guarantee is not a safe alternative and is not taken; the agent moves to the next rung or escalates instead.
- **Rung 3 — Rollback.** Where in-place self-correction is unavailable or insufficient, the agent reverses the change to its last known-good state. The rollback path, its triggers, and its time-to-recover target are owned by `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §5–§7; the ladder invokes that rollback and redefines none of it. A rollback proceeds autonomously only within the bounds that document and §7 set; a rollback that cannot restore the last known-good state within its bounds, or could only complete by breaching a guarantee, escalates (`release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §8).
- **Rung 4 — Human escalation.** Where retry, a safe alternative, and a rollback are each unavailable, insufficient, or exhausted, the agent escalates. How the escalation is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved is owned by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`; the containment-first recovery that follows a declared incident is owned by `incident-response-and-recovery.md`, which may itself invoke a rollback or a self-correction as a recovery action. Escalation is the terminal rung and is not itself a self-correction attempt bounded by §7.

The following rules bind every rung:

- **The ladder never compounds harm.** No rung worsens the condition it exists to resolve, spreads it to further tenants, regions, or tiers, or breaches a guarantee to reach recovery; consistent with the containment-first ordering of `incident-response-and-recovery.md` §5, a correction that could only proceed by causing a worse condition is refused, and the agent escalates instead. Recovery never comes at the cost of a guarantee.
- **The order is followed, never skipped to over-correct or to avoid escalating.** A broader rung is reached only where the narrower ones are unavailable or insufficient; the agent does not reach for a rollback where a retry would recover, nor retry indefinitely to avoid a rollback or an escalation. A signal that forbids a correction (§8) bypasses the ladder entirely — it goes straight to halt-and-escalate, never to a rung.
- **Each rung re-clears every precondition and is a traced, consequential action.** Every attempt satisfies §4 anew and produces the agent-action record of `audit-and-traceability.md` §6; a rung whose advance cannot be attributed and reconstructed has not, for this document, recovered.
- **Each rung stays within the outer bounds.** Every attempt counts against the iteration ceiling and draws against the envelope; whichever outer bound, or the attempt ceiling of §7, is reached first ends the ladder and escalates (§8).
- **A rung that leaves an unsafe or unattributable state is not a recovery.** A correction that corrupts state, cannot establish its provenance, or produces an unsafe resume condition (`agent-state-and-memory-spec.md` §6, §7) has not recovered; it halts and escalates rather than advancing to a further rung.

## 7. Maximum Self-Correction Attempts Before Mandatory Escalation

The agent may make only a finite number of autonomous correction attempts before escalation becomes mandatory. This ceiling is this document's own rule; it is the bound the governing documents cite this document as owning, and it is what keeps a recovery from continuing indefinitely.

- **A finite ceiling is fixed before recovery begins.** The maximum number of autonomous self-correction attempts the agent may make on a given error — across retry, safe alternative, and rollback — is finite and established in advance, not discovered or extended while recovery runs. A recovery without a fixed attempt ceiling does not proceed autonomously.
- **The concrete value is a number owned elsewhere.** This document requires that the ceiling exist, be finite, and bind every rung; the specific numeric value it takes is a non-functional ceiling owned by `non-functional-requirements.md`, keyed to here without minting a number of its own — consistent with how the maximum iterations and retries of `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5 key their value to that document.
- **The attempt ceiling is a nested bound within the outer bounds.** It bounds the ladder from within; the iteration and retry ceiling of `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5 and the per-task and per-session envelopes of `token-and-compute-budget.md` §5 bound it from without. Every attempt is an iteration and a draw against those outer bounds; whichever bound — the attempt ceiling or an outer one — is reached first ends autonomous correction and escalates.
- **A repeated ineffective correction is not a fresh attempt to spend.** A retry against an unchanged condition, or a correction that makes no traceable progress toward recovery, is a no-op under `agent-loop-constraints.md` §7 and escalates rather than consuming the ceiling; repeated ineffective reaction is itself a signal that mandates escalation (`observability-and-monitoring.md` §7, §8).
- **Reaching the ceiling ends autonomy; it is never widened to recover.** When the maximum attempts is reached, autonomous correction stops and the agent escalates for human decision. The ceiling is never raised, reset, or waived mid-recovery, and a recovery is never split across attempts, tasks, or sessions to evade it, consistent with the anti-splitting rules of `agent-loop-constraints.md` §5, `token-and-compute-budget.md` §5, and `agent-state-and-memory-spec.md` §7.
- **Escalation past the exhausted ceiling is mandatory, not optional.** Reaching the bound mandates human escalation; a further autonomous attempt past it is forbidden (`agent-operating-charter.md` §7, §8), and the exhausted bound is itself an enumerated approval-gate trigger (`human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` §5).

## 8. Conditions That Forbid Further Autonomous Action

The conditions that forbid any further autonomous action are the obligation that gives every rule above its force. Each resolves in one direction: toward stopping. Under any of the following, no further autonomous action — no rung, no retry, no correction — may be taken; the agent halts and escalates. The list is explicit and deny-by-default; a condition need match only one entry to forbid further autonomous action.

- **A breach, or a possible breach, of an invariant.** A correction that would violate, has violated, or cannot be shown not to violate an invariant of `system-invariants.md` §4 halts and escalates immediately per that document's §3; it is never resolved by a further rung, retried against the same breach, or worked around.
- **The attempt ceiling or an outer bound is reached.** Reaching the maximum self-correction attempts (§7), the iteration or retry ceiling (`agent-loop-constraints.md` §8), or an exhausted token, compute, or cost envelope (`token-and-compute-budget.md` §8) forbids further autonomous correction and mandates escalation.
- **An escalation-mandating signal or a prohibited unilateral recovery condition.** An escalation-mandating runtime signal or a monitoring blind spot (`observability-and-monitoring.md` §8), a mandatory human escalation threshold, or any action the incident response forbids as a prohibited unilateral recovery (`incident-response-and-recovery.md` §7, §8) forbids further autonomous action; the recovery is handed to that response, not continued.
- **A correction that could only proceed by breaching a guarantee.** Where no available rung can recover without relaxing an invariant, a severity threshold, a residency obligation, a quality floor, or the reversibility guarantee (INV-08), no further autonomous action is taken; recovery never comes at the cost of a guarantee, and the agent escalates instead.
- **A corrupt, unattributable, or unsafe-to-resume state.** A correction that has corrupted state, cannot establish its provenance, or has produced an unsafe resume condition (`agent-state-and-memory-spec.md` §6, §7) forbids further autonomous action on that state; it is re-verified or escalated, never worked around.
- **Missing or stale context.** Where the context a correction requires is missing or can no longer be shown current (`prompt-and-context-management.md` §8), no further autonomous correction proceeds on it; the agent re-derives what it needs or halts, and never retries against stale context.
- **An action requiring approval, or an unresolvable conflict.** Where a correction matches any approval-gate trigger (`human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` §5), or meets a conflict the precedence order of `agent-operating-charter.md` §4 cannot resolve — including a conflict among invariants of equal standing (`system-invariants.md` §6) — autonomy stops and the decision is handed to a human.
- **Uncertainty about whether further action is safe.** Where it cannot be established that a further correction is within every bound and guarantee above, no further autonomous action is taken; the condition is treated as forbidding it, and the agent halts and escalates, consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3.

- **A halt on any forbidding condition is a consequential action and is recorded.** It produces the audit record `audit-and-traceability.md` §4 requires — including the condition that forbade further action and the state of the work when it stopped — exactly as any other halt-and-escalate event does.
- **The prohibition is non-negotiable and always-on.** It binds every lifecycle phase, every tenant, and every region, and is never suspended for urgency, for apparent proximity to recovery, or for a low-apparent-risk correction; no level of authority waives it, and no forbidding condition is resolved by continuing.

## 9. Precedence and Ownership Boundaries

When a rule in this document meets any other consideration, it is resolved by the precedence order of `agent-operating-charter.md` §4, at whose apex the Vision and Charter sits.

- **The charter prevails.** No rule in this document is applied in a way that contradicts the Vision and Charter; where this document appears to conflict with the charter, the charter governs.
- **This document grants no authority a higher layer withholds.** The ladder here only recovers within bounds and stops autonomous action sooner; it never extends recovery past a bound another document sets, and never confers authority the invariants or the guardrail, architecture, and delivery layers withhold.
- **A guarantee is never spent to recover.** No invariant, floor, severity threshold, or residency obligation is relaxed to make a correction succeed or a recovery complete; a recovery bought at the cost of a guarantee is never a net gain.

This document owns the error- and regression-detection signals that trigger a correction, the ordered fallback ladder, the maximum self-correction attempts before mandatory escalation, and the conditions that forbid any further autonomous action. It does not own the specifics other documents govern, and none of those documents may weaken this model:

- **The invariants themselves** — their binary form, the blocking-check rule, and the halt-and-escalate rule — are owned by `system-invariants.md`; this document holds every correction attempt to them and redefines none.
- **The preconditions for autonomous action, the document-precedence order, the permitted and approval-required action classes, and the duty to halt and escalate** are owned by `agent-operating-charter.md`; this document binds every attempt to those preconditions and resolves its conflicts by that precedence order without restating either.
- **The mandatory audit events, immutability and tamper-evidence, agent-action logging, and minimum traceability** are owned by `audit-and-traceability.md`; this document records every attempt, halt, and escalation to that standard without restating its elements.
- **The required metrics, the alert thresholds and runtime severity, the health signals that trigger self-correction or rollback, and the escalation-mandating signals** are owned by `observability-and-monitoring.md`; this document responds to those signals and never redefines one or its threshold.

## 10. Binding Rules

These rules hold for every recovery the agent runs subject to this model and are subordinate to the charter.

- **An error or regression is detected from a signal owned elsewhere; the response is owned here.** The agent recognizes its own error only through a signal it never redefines; uncertainty about whether its own work is in error resolves toward recovery, never toward proceeding on it.
- **Every correction attempt clears the preconditions of autonomy.** Each is granted, in scope, invariant-holding, reversible, and minimally traceable, and draws against the outer bounds; an attempt that cannot satisfy a precondition does not proceed and escalates.
- **Recovery follows the ordered ladder — retry, safe alternative, rollback, human escalation.** A broader rung is reached only where the narrower ones are unavailable or insufficient; the order is never skipped to over-correct or to avoid escalating.
- **The ladder never compounds harm.** No rung worsens or spreads the condition, breaches a guarantee to recover, or leaves an unsafe or unrecoverable state; a correction that could only proceed by causing a worse condition is refused, and the agent escalates.
- **A rollback is invoked, not redefined.** The ladder invokes the rollback of `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` as one rung and hands an exhausted or forbidden recovery to `incident-response-and-recovery.md`; neither is redefined here.
- **A finite maximum of self-correction attempts binds every recovery.** The attempt ceiling is fixed in advance and keyed to `non-functional-requirements.md`; a repeated ineffective correction earns no fresh attempt, and reaching the ceiling — or an outer bound — ends autonomy and escalates.
- **The ceiling is never widened to recover.** It is never raised, reset, or waived mid-recovery, and a recovery is never split across attempts, tasks, or sessions to evade it.
- **The conditions that forbid further autonomous action are explicit and honored.** An invariant breach, an exhausted bound, an escalation-mandating signal, a prohibited unilateral recovery, a guarantee-breaching correction, a corrupt or unsafe-to-resume state, missing or stale context, an approval trigger, an unresolvable conflict, and any uncertainty each stop autonomy and escalate.
- **Every attempt, halt, and escalation is traceable and attributable.** Each produces the record `audit-and-traceability.md` requires, including the condition that ended autonomous recovery and the state of the work when it stopped.
- **The signals, numeric values, and escalation mechanics are owned elsewhere.** This document requires that the agent recover predictably within bounds and stop where it must; the detection signals, the concrete numbers, and the escalation mechanics are owned by the documents assigned to them.
- **The builder / built separation holds throughout.** This document binds the agent's own platform-layer self-correction; the error handling a builder configures inside their own built artifact remains the builder's own.
- **Everything remains domain-neutral and platform-level.** No detection rule, ladder rung, attempt ceiling, or forbidding condition in this document encodes the characteristics of any single domain; all remain valid for the platform, in any tenant and any region.
