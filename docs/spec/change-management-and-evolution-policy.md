# Change Management and Evolution Policy — AI ahaMatic

This document defines **how the platform evolves over time without violating a guarantee it has already made or accumulating unmanaged drift** — the backward-compatibility obligation that governs every change to the platform, the rules by which a capability, contract, or schema is deprecated and a migration is carried out along a managed, announced path, the documentation update that must accompany every change so the governing library never drifts from the platform it governs, and the changes that may not be adopted without human review. It states **what** an evolution must preserve, announce, and update, and when it must stop for review; it does not describe how any change, deprecation, migration, rollback, review, or documentation update is scheduled, orchestrated, or tooled.

This is an Evolution-phase artifact — the eighth and final document of the Meta-Operations domain, the concluding Evolution-phase artifact of that domain, and the final document of the AI ahaMatic documentation library; it closes the Evolution tier of the pyramid, to which every prior document deferred its evolution, deprecation, and migration mechanics. It inherits its framing from the Vision and Charter and is subordinate to it; where it appears to conflict with the charter, the charter prevails. It operates entirely within the authority model of `agent-operating-charter.md`: every change it governs is subject to that document's preconditions for autonomous action (§5), its permitted-action and approval classes (§6, §7), and its duty to halt and escalate (§8), and every conflict this document meets is resolved by that document's canonical precedence order (§4). It holds every invariant of `system-invariants.md` §4 continuously, chiefly the two guarantees that survive evolution — **INV-08 (reversibility and recovery)**, under which no change advances the platform into a state it cannot safely leave, and **INV-09 (backward-compatible evolution)**, under which no change breaks a guarantee already made to an existing builder or built artifact except along a managed, announced path — and it makes the minimum traceability of `audit-and-traceability.md` §7 and the agent-action logging of its §6 a per-change obligation. It treats the API-specific versioning, deprecation, and backward-compatibility rules of `api-contract-spec.md` §4 and §5 and the contract-change sign-off of its §7, the stored-data migration-safety and backward-compatibility rules of `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §7 and §8, the release-level rollback path and recovery target of `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §5–§7, the ordered fallback ladder, maximum self-correction attempts, and conditions that forbid further autonomous action of `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md` as the recovery path for a failed or reverted evolution, the third-party dependency and license policy of `legal-and-licensing-constraints.md` §4–§7, the fixed architectural decisions of `architecture-overview.md` §7, and the approval-gate triggers and default-to-halt of `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` as the machinery a human review is carried out through — citing each, redefining none. Every rule here is **platform-level and domain-neutral** — it holds for any component, any tenant, any region, and any lifecycle phase, regardless of what any builder builds with the platform.

This document owns what the governing documents deferred to it for evolving the platform without violating prior guarantees or accumulating unmanaged drift: **the platform-wide backward-compatibility obligation that governs all change**, **the deprecation and migration rules and the managed, announced path they follow**, **the documentation-update requirement that binds every change**, and **the changes that require human review before adoption**. It does not own the invariants themselves (`system-invariants.md`), the API-specific backward-compatibility, versioning, and deprecation elaboration (`api-contract-spec.md`), the stored-data migration-safety and backward-compatibility elaboration (`data-model-and-entity-spec.md`), the release-level rollback mechanics and recovery target (`release-and-rollback-protocol.md`), the fallback ladder and its attempt ceiling (`self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md`), the license categories and dependency policy (`legal-and-licensing-constraints.md`), the fixed architectural decisions (`architecture-overview.md`), the mandatory audit events, immutability, and minimum traceability (`audit-and-traceability.md`), the preconditions, precedence order, and duty to halt (`agent-operating-charter.md`), the concrete numeric values (`non-functional-requirements.md`), or how any approval, escalation, or halt is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved (`human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`) — each remains owned by the document already assigned to it, cited throughout, never redefined here.

## 1. Purpose and Reading Order

The document answers four questions:

- **What a managed change, a prior guarantee, backward compatibility, deprecation, migration, and drift are**, and how a broken prior guarantee, unmanaged drift, and documentation drift differ from one another and from an invariant, a rollback, a self-correction, and an incident.
- **What backward compatibility obligates of every change**, so that no change silently breaks a guarantee the platform has already made.
- **What deprecation and migration require**, so that a capability, contract, or schema is retired or moved only along an announced, reversible, time-bounded path, and what documentation must accompany every change so the governing library never drifts from the platform.
- **What changes may not be adopted without human review** — where evolution stops for a human decision, absolutely and without waiver.

It is structured as a pyramid: first the concepts of an evolution and the failure modes they guard against, then the preconditions every change inherits, then the backward-compatibility obligation that governs all change, then the deprecation and migration rules and the documentation update that keep evolution managed and the library current, then the changes that require human review and give the whole its force.

---

## 2. Scope of This Model

Change management and evolution are properties of the platform's own evolution, not of any one thing built on it. The model applies along three dimensions at once.

- **Bound to platform-layer evolution.** This document governs how the platform core, builder tooling, and the governing library itself change over time. What change management a builder applies to their own built artifact — how a built application versions, deprecates, or migrates its own content — is builder-defined content and is never absorbed into this document; the builder / built separation holds for change management exactly as it holds for every other primitive (INV-06). The obligations here bind the platform's own evolution alone, never satisfied or excused by the change practices of any single built application.
- **Across every lifecycle phase.** The policy binds continuously — Evolution primarily, but a backward-compatibility obligation, a managed path, or a documentation update is not relaxed because the change arose in an earlier phase. A change proposed at design, generated at execution, promoted at release, or introduced by a self-correction is each subject to the same obligations, exactly as the invariants the agent never breaches are (`system-invariants.md` §5).
- **Across every tenant and every region.** Every backward-compatibility obligation, deprecation rule, migration constraint, documentation requirement, and review trigger holds identically for each tenant and in each operating region; no evolution is weakened, skipped, or narrowed for one tenant's or one region's convenience. Consistent with tenant isolation (INV-01) and residency (INV-07), a change affecting one tenant never observes, affects, or discloses another's state, and never moves data across a region boundary in violation of residency.

The model is domain-neutral: every backward-compatibility obligation, deprecation and migration rule, documentation requirement, and review trigger is expressed in terms that hold regardless of what is built on the platform, and none presumes the characteristics of any single domain.

## 3. What an Evolution, Backward Compatibility, and Drift Are

The core objective of this document rests on a small set of concepts that must not be conflated.

- **A change** is any alteration to the platform core, builder tooling, or the governing library — a capability, a contract, a schema, a dependency, a configuration, or a governing document.
- **A prior guarantee** is any commitment the platform has already made and an existing builder or built artifact may rely on — an API contract (`api-contract-spec.md`), a stored-data guarantee (`data-model-and-entity-spec.md`), an invariant, a quality floor, or a published behavior.
- **Backward compatibility** is the property that a change leaves every prior guarantee to an existing builder or built artifact intact, or breaks it only along a managed, announced path; it is INV-09 stated as an obligation on every change.
- **Deprecation** is the announced, time-bounded state in which a capability, contract, or schema is marked for retirement while its guarantees continue to be honored, before it is withdrawn.
- **A migration** is the movement of a builder, a built artifact, or committed data and its schema from a prior state to a new one, preserving what must be preserved throughout.
- **The managed path** is the announced, reversible, time-bounded, reviewed route a guarantee-affecting change follows, so that an existing builder or built artifact has time to adapt (C-17) and the change can be reversed if it fails.
- **Drift** is unmanaged divergence — a change that leaves the platform no longer holding a guarantee it made, or the governing library no longer describing the platform it governs, accumulated because a change was not managed or not documented.

This document guards against three distinct failure modes, each of which the rules below are shaped to prevent:

| Failure Mode | What It Is | Primarily Guarded By |
|---|---|---|
| Broken prior guarantee | A change breaks a guarantee already made to an existing builder or built artifact without following the managed, announced path. | The backward-compatibility obligation (§5). |
| Unmanaged drift | A capability, contract, schema, or dependency deprecated, migrated, or retired without an announced, reversible, time-bounded managed path — or a change adopted that the platform then no longer holds. | The deprecation and migration rules (§6). |
| Documentation drift | The governing library falling out of step with the platform it governs, so the corpus no longer reconstructs the platform's actual guarantees or ownership boundaries. | The documentation-update requirements (§7). |

The changes that require human review (§8) give all three guards their force: a broken guarantee, an unmanaged deprecation or migration, and an undocumented change each stop for a human decision before adoption rather than being taken autonomously.

These are distinct from several related instruments, each owned elsewhere and never redefined here:

| Concept | What It Is | Owned By |
|---|---|---|
| System invariant | A binary property that must always hold; its breach halts execution and escalates. | `system-invariants.md` |
| API-contract backward compatibility, versioning, and deprecation | The contract-specific elaboration of INV-09 and its versioning and deprecation policy. | `api-contract-spec.md` §4, §5 |
| Stored-data migration-safety and backward compatibility | The data-specific elaboration of INV-08 and INV-09 for schemas and committed data. | `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §7, §8 |
| Rollback | The reversal of a released change to its last known-good state. | `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` |
| Self-correction ladder and its maximum attempts | The ordered recovery for a failed or reverted change, and the attempt ceiling that bounds it. | `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md` |
| License categories and dependency policy | What license a dependency may carry and what a dependency change re-triggers. | `legal-and-licensing-constraints.md` |
| Approval gate, escalation, and default-to-halt | How a human review is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved, and the ambiguity default. | `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` |
| Platform-wide backward-compatibility obligation, deprecation and migration policy, documentation-update requirement, and change-management review triggers | The obligations that keep evolution managed and the library current. | This document |

Two boundaries are load-bearing and are preserved throughout:

- **This document owns the platform-wide policy; it never redefines an invariant, a domain-specific elaboration, a rollback, or an approval.** The platform-wide backward-compatibility obligation, the deprecation and migration policy, and the managed path are owned here; the API-specific backward-compatibility, versioning, and deprecation rules are owned by `api-contract-spec.md`, the stored-data migration-safety and backward-compatibility rules by `data-model-and-entity-spec.md`, the invariants by `system-invariants.md`, the rollback the managed path may invoke by `release-and-rollback-protocol.md`, the recovery for a failed change by `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md`, and the review mechanics by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`. This document requires that every change be backward-compatible or managed, that every deprecation and migration follow the path, and that every change be documented and reviewed where required; it redefines none of the elaborations or mechanics it depends on.
- **The governing library must not drift from the platform it governs.** The documents in `docs/` are the source of the platform's guarantees and ownership boundaries; a change to the platform is complete only when the governing documents it alters are updated to match it (§7). A platform that has changed while its governing library has not is a platform whose guarantees can no longer be reconstructed from their record — a form of drift this document exists to prevent.

## 4. Preconditions Bind Every Change

Because each change this policy governs is either an autonomous action or a human-directed one carried out by the agent, every precondition of `agent-operating-charter.md` §5 binds it. Evolving the platform confers no authority the underlying change would otherwise lack, and the need to evolve is never a licence to act outside those preconditions.

- **Each change is within granted authority and in scope.** A change falls within the agent's granted authority and the authorized scope of `vision-and-charter.md` §3, admits no domain content into the core and tunes no primitive toward one vertical (INV-05, INV-06), and never pursues a non-goal of its §4; the need to evolve is never an occasion to admit domain content or privilege a vertical.
- **Each change holds every invariant.** A change is checked against the full invariant set of `system-invariants.md` §4 and breaches none; a change that would produce or sustain a breach does not proceed, and a breach detected mid-change halts and escalates immediately under that document's §3.
- **Each change retains a reversible, recoverable path.** No change advances the platform into a state it cannot safely leave (INV-08); a change without a defined, reversible path is not permitted, and the release-level mechanics of that path are owned by `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §5 and never redefined here. An evolution is never adopted on the expectation that a rollback path will be defined afterward.
- **Each change leaves its trace.** Every change establishes the minimum traceability of `audit-and-traceability.md` §7 — its attribution, the invariants and gates evaluated, its reversible path, its backward-compatibility result, any required approval, and its outcome — and produces the agent-action record of that document's §6. A change that cannot be attributed and reconstructed is itself a traceability violation, escalated under `agent-operating-charter.md` §5.
- **A change that cannot clear a precondition does not proceed.** Where any precondition above cannot be established, the change is not taken autonomously; the agent halts and escalates (§8), consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3.

## 5. The Backward-Compatibility Obligation

Every change to the platform preserves every prior guarantee made to an existing builder or built artifact, or breaks it only along the managed, announced path of §6. This is the platform-wide obligation that governs all change; it is INV-09 stated as a duty on every evolution, and it is the obligation `api-contract-spec.md` §5 and `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §8 each elaborate for their own slice.

- **The obligation spans every kind of prior guarantee, not only contracts and stored data.** Backward compatibility is owed to an existing consumer of an API contract (`api-contract-spec.md` §5), to previously committed data under an evolving schema (`data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §8), and equally to any invariant, quality floor, or published behavior an existing builder or built artifact relies on. This document owns the platform-wide obligation that governs all of them; the invariant it enforces is owned by `system-invariants.md`, and the contract- and data-specific elaborations are owned by the two documents named, which this document cites and never redefines.
- **Backward compatibility is judged by effect on an existing consumer, never by the intent or scope of the change.** A change is backward-compatible only where every existing builder and built artifact continues to hold what it was already guaranteed, unchanged; a change is breaking where it would cause an existing, correctly-relying consumer to fail, misbehave, or receive a materially different result than was guaranteed — consistent with `api-contract-spec.md` §5 and `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §8, neither redefined here.
- **A change that would break a guarantee outside the managed path does not proceed.** A break of a prior guarantee is permitted only along the announced, reviewed, reversible path of §6; a change that would break a guarantee unannounced or unmanaged is refused, exactly as gate **G-6** of `prd.md` §5 requires and as `agent-operating-charter.md` §7 holds.
- **Uncertainty about whether a change is backward-compatible resolves to treating it as breaking.** Where it cannot be established that every existing builder and built artifact remains unaffected, the change is treated as backward-incompatible and follows the managed path — the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3 applied to evolution, consistent with `api-contract-spec.md` §5 and `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §8.
- **A guarantee is never spent to evolve.** Backward compatibility is never preserved by relaxing another guarantee — data integrity, tenant isolation, authorization, residency, or a quality floor — and no other guarantee is relaxed to make a change compatible; where compatibility and another guarantee cannot both hold by any available change, the conflict halts and escalates per `system-invariants.md` §3 and §6 rather than resolving by relaxing either.
- **A change carries its reversible path before it proceeds.** Consistent with INV-08, a change that alters a prior guarantee retains a defined, reversible path back to the prior state before it is adopted; the release-level mechanics of that path are owned by `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §5, and a change reaching a tier that is found to break a guarantee outside the managed path is a rollback trigger there (`release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §6), neither redefined here.

## 6. Deprecation and Migration Rules

Where a change must retire, replace, or move a prior guarantee, it does so only along a managed, announced, time-bounded, reversible path. This document owns the platform-wide deprecation and migration policy and the managed path — the policy `api-contract-spec.md` §4 and §10 and `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §8 and §9 each defer to it — and it invokes, never redefines, the contract-specific and data-specific mechanics those documents own.

- **Deprecation is an announced, time-bounded state, never a silent withdrawal.** A capability, contract, or schema entering deprecation is explicitly marked as such before it is withdrawn, continues to honor its guarantees for the duration of the deprecation, and is withdrawn only after that duration elapses — consistent with the versioning and deprecation policy `api-contract-spec.md` §4 owns for contracts. This document owns the general scheduling and communication of that managed path across every kind of change; the contract-specific version rules remain owned by `api-contract-spec.md`.
- **The managed path gives existing builders and built artifacts time to adapt.** The path is announced before it takes effect, bounds a finite window during which the prior guarantee continues to hold, and completes only after the window elapses (C-17); it is never compressed to a point where an existing consumer has no opportunity to adapt, because a zero-length adaptation window is a silent break, not a managed one.
- **A migration preserves what must be preserved and is reversible throughout.** A migration of committed data or a schema holds validity, consistency, referential integrity, and tenant isolation throughout, and completes only along a reversible, all-or-nothing path — the migration-safety constraints owned by `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §7. This document owns the policy that a guarantee-affecting migration follows the managed path; the safety constraints and the stored-data backward-compatibility determination remain owned by `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §7 and §8 and are never redefined here.
- **A failed or reverted evolution recovers along the paths other documents own.** Where a deprecation or migration fails, cannot complete, or must be reversed, recovery follows the ordered fallback ladder, the maximum self-correction attempts, and the conditions that forbid further autonomous action of `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md`, and the rollback to the last known-good state of `release-and-rollback-protocol.md` §5–§7; this document treats those as the recovery path for a failed evolution and redefines neither.
- **A dependency change is re-evaluated for its license along the same path.** Updating, replacing, or newly combining a third-party dependency re-triggers the license category and combination check of `legal-and-licensing-constraints.md` §5; a change that would introduce a prohibited or unconfirmed license is a blocking license-incompatibility under that document's §7 and is refused, never carried through a deprecation or migration window. This document owns the change-management path a dependency change travels; the license policy it must satisfy is owned by `legal-and-licensing-constraints.md`.
- **The concrete durations are numeric values owned elsewhere.** This document requires that a deprecation window and a migration window each be finite, announced, and fixed in advance; the specific numeric values — the length of an adaptation window, an acceptable migration duration or downtime — are owned by `non-functional-requirements.md`, keyed to here without minting a number of its own.
- **Uncertainty about whether a change is backward-incompatible resolves to the managed path.** Where it cannot be established that a change leaves every prior guarantee intact, it is treated as backward-incompatible and follows the managed path rather than proceeding as an ordinary change — the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3 applied to deprecation and migration.

## 7. Documentation-Update Requirements Accompanying Every Change

The governing library is the source of the platform's guarantees, and the platform must never drift from it. This document owns the requirement that every change be accompanied by the corresponding update to the governing documents it alters, so the library continues to describe the platform it governs.

- **A change and the documentation of it are one unit.** Where a change alters what a governing document in `docs/` records — a guarantee, a rule, a boundary, or an ownership assignment — the corresponding update to that document is part of the same change, not a deferred follow-up. A change whose governing-document update has not been made is incomplete, exactly as a release without a defined rollback path is incomplete (INV-08).
- **The library remains the reconstructable record of the platform's guarantees.** After a change, the governing documents must still establish what the platform guarantees and where each guarantee is owned; a platform that has changed while its documents have not can no longer have its guarantees reconstructed from their record, which is the documentation form of the drift this document exists to prevent, and which the reconstructability requirement of `audit-and-traceability.md` §3 depends on.
- **Ownership remains singular and unambiguous.** Where a change moves, adds, or removes an ownership boundary between documents, both the ceding and the receiving document are updated together, so that every rule remains owned by exactly one document and no rule is left owned by two or by none. A change never silently relocates what a document owns.
- **The corpus remains internally non-contradictory.** A change never leaves two governing documents asserting conflicting rules; an apparent conflict that the precedence order of `agent-operating-charter.md` §4 exists to resolve is a governed relationship, but a contradiction introduced by an un-updated document is a defect to be reconciled as part of the change, not a precedence question to be deferred.
- **The documentation updates are verified for cross-document consistency before the change is complete.** Making the documentation updates a change requires is not sufficient on its own; the updated library is verified to be internally consistent — the update is propagated to every document that references what changed, every enumeration and count still agrees library-wide, every cross-reference still resolves to the section it names, and the corpus introduces no contradiction — before the change is considered complete. A change whose documentation has been updated but not verified for self-consistency is incomplete, exactly as a change whose governing-document update has not been made is incomplete. This obligation is documentation self-consistency, distinct from the platform-versus-library consistency the requirements above secure: the former guards the library against drifting from the platform, this guards the library against drifting from itself.
- **The documentation update is itself a governed, traceable change.** Updating a governing document is a consequential change subject to this policy in full — it holds every invariant, retains a reversible path, and produces the minimum trace of `audit-and-traceability.md` §7; a change to a governing document's owned scope is itself a change requiring the human review of §8.
- **This binds the platform's own governing library, not a builder's own documentation.** The requirement governs the documents in `docs/` that govern the platform; how a builder documents their own built artifact is builder-defined content and is never absorbed into this document (INV-06).
- **Uncertainty about whether the library still describes the platform resolves against proceeding.** Where it cannot be established that the governing documents still describe the platform after a change, the platform is treated as having drifted; the change is reconciled with its documentation or does not proceed, consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3.

## 8. Changes That Require Human Review Before Adoption

The changes that require human review before adoption are the obligation that gives every rule above its force. Each resolves in one direction: toward stopping for a human decision before the change is adopted. This document owns the change-management-level review triggers; the underlying condition of each is owned by the document named, and how a review is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved is owned by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`. The list is explicit and deny-by-default; a change need match only one entry to require review before adoption.

- **A backward-incompatible change entering the managed path.** A change that breaks, or cannot be shown not to break, a prior guarantee to an existing builder or built artifact (INV-09) carries the human sign-off that authorizes the managed path to begin — the contract-change sign-off of `api-contract-spec.md` §7 for a contract, and the approval class of `agent-operating-charter.md` §7 for any other guarantee. A change that would break a guarantee outside the managed path does not proceed at all.
- **A deprecation, withdrawal, or retirement of a capability, contract, or schema.** Committing to a deprecation timeline affects every existing consumer of what is deprecated; the commitment is one human judgment must authorize, consistent with the deprecation and withdrawal sign-off of `api-contract-spec.md` §7 and the controlled-change and deprecation capability (C-17).
- **A migration affecting committed data or stored-data backward compatibility.** A migration that changes committed data or determines a schema evolution to be backward-incompatible carries review before adoption, consistent with the migration-safety and stored-data backward-compatibility rules of `data-model-and-entity-spec.md` §7 and §8.
- **A change to a fixed architectural decision.** A change to any decision fixed by `architecture-overview.md` §7 is a governed change requiring review, never a unilateral design choice.
- **A dependency change that introduces or alters a license obligation.** A dependency change carrying a new or altered license obligation carries the review the license policy of `legal-and-licensing-constraints.md` §5 and §7 requires; a change that would introduce a prohibited or unconfirmed license is a blocking license-incompatibility refused outright under that document's §7, not a change reviewed into adoption.
- **A change to a governing document's owned scope.** A change that alters what a governing document owns, or the boundary between two documents (§7), is itself a governed change requiring review, so the library's ownership structure is never rearranged unilaterally.
- **Any change matching an enumerated approval-gate trigger.** A change matching any approval-gate trigger of `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` §5 or any approval class of `agent-operating-charter.md` §7 — including any promotion to Production, any residency-relevant action, and any action carrying an unresolved Critical or High vulnerability — stops for the review that trigger requires; satisfying one trigger never excuses another.
- **Uncertainty about whether review is required.** Where it cannot be established that a change falls outside every entry above, it is treated as requiring review, consistent with the deny-by-default rule of `system-invariants.md` §3.

The following rules bind every review:

- **Review precedes adoption, never follows it.** A change requiring review is not adopted and then reviewed; the review is obtained before the change takes effect, from a safe, reversible state, exactly as `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` §6 requires of every gated action.
- **A review authorizes a managed, in-bounds path, never a waiver.** A human review may authorize a change to proceed along the managed path within stated bounds; it never waives an invariant, a floor, or a backward-compatibility guarantee, and a change that could proceed only by breaching a guarantee is refused, not reviewed into adoption (`human-in-the-loop-protocol.md` §7; `system-invariants.md` §6).
- **A declined or reverted change recovers along the owned paths.** Where a review declines a change, or an adopted change must be reverted, recovery follows the fallback ladder and attempt ceiling of `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md` and the rollback of `release-and-rollback-protocol.md`; neither is redefined here.
- **The requirement is non-negotiable and always-on.** It binds every lifecycle phase, every tenant, and every region, and is never suspended for urgency, for apparent proximity to completion, or for a low-apparent-risk change. A review decision, and any halt on an ambiguous trigger, is itself a consequential action producing the audit record `audit-and-traceability.md` §4 requires.

## 9. Precedence and Ownership Boundaries

When a rule in this document meets any other consideration, it is resolved by the precedence order of `agent-operating-charter.md` §4, at whose apex the Vision and Charter sits.

- **The charter prevails.** No rule in this document is applied in a way that contradicts the Vision and Charter; where this document appears to conflict with the charter, the charter governs.
- **This document grants no authority a higher layer withholds.** The policy here only constrains how the platform evolves and stops a change for review where it must; it never extends a change past a bound another document sets, and never confers authority the invariants or the guardrail, architecture, and delivery layers withhold.
- **A guarantee is never spent to evolve.** No invariant, floor, severity threshold, residency obligation, or prior guarantee is relaxed to make a change succeed, ship, or complete; an evolution bought at the cost of a guarantee is never a net gain.

This document owns the platform-wide backward-compatibility obligation that governs all change, the deprecation and migration rules and the managed path they follow, the documentation-update requirement that binds every change, and the changes that require human review before adoption. It does not own the specifics other documents govern, and none of those documents may weaken this model:

- **The invariants themselves** — their binary form, the blocking-check rule, and the halt-and-escalate rule, including INV-08 and INV-09 — are owned by `system-invariants.md`; this document holds every change to them and redefines none.
- **The preconditions for autonomous action, the document-precedence order, the approval classes, and the duty to halt and escalate** are owned by `agent-operating-charter.md`; this document binds every change to those preconditions and resolves its conflicts by that precedence order without restating either.
- **The API-specific versioning and deprecation policy, the contract backward-compatibility rules, and the contract-change sign-off** are owned by `api-contract-spec.md`; this document owns the platform-wide obligation and managed path those rules elaborate for contracts, and redefines none of them.
- **The stored-data migration-safety constraints and the stored-data backward-compatibility requirements** are owned by `data-model-and-entity-spec.md`; this document owns the platform-wide policy those rules elaborate for stored data, and redefines none of them.
- **The release strategy, the rollback path and triggers, and the recovery target** are owned by `release-and-rollback-protocol.md`; this document treats a rollback as the release-level reversal the managed path may invoke and never redefines it.
- **The ordered fallback ladder, the maximum self-correction attempts, and the conditions that forbid further autonomous action** are owned by `self-correction-and-fallback-protocol.md`; this document treats that ladder as the recovery path for a failed or reverted evolution and never redefines it.
- **The permitted and prohibited license categories, the third-party dependency policy, the attribution requirements, and license-incompatibility as a blocking check** are owned by `legal-and-licensing-constraints.md`; this document routes a dependency change through its policy and never redefines it.
- **The fixed architectural decisions and the layered separation** are owned by `architecture-overview.md`; this document requires review to change one without redefining the decision.
- **The mandatory audit events, immutability and tamper-evidence, agent-action logging, and minimum traceability** are owned by `audit-and-traceability.md`; this document records every change, deprecation, migration, documentation update, and review to that standard without restating its elements.
- **The concrete numeric values** — deprecation-window and migration durations, and every other quantified floor a change is held to — are owned by `non-functional-requirements.md`; this document requires that each window be finite, announced, and fixed in advance, and keys to those values without minting a number.
- **The enumerated approval-gate triggers, and how a review, approval, or escalation is requested, routed, recorded, and resolved, and the default to halt on ambiguity** are owned by `human-in-the-loop-protocol.md`; this document establishes the change-management-level triggers that require review, not the mechanics that carry it out.
- **What change management a builder applies to their own built artifact** is builder-defined content and is never governed by this document; this document binds the platform's own evolution and its governing library only.

## 10. Binding Rules

These rules hold for every change the platform undergoes subject to this model and are subordinate to the charter.

- **Every change preserves every prior guarantee, or breaks it only along the managed path.** Backward compatibility is judged by effect on an existing builder or built artifact, never by intent; uncertainty resolves to treating a change as breaking, and a break outside the managed path does not proceed.
- **Every change clears the preconditions of autonomy.** Each is granted, in scope, invariant-holding, reversible, and minimally traceable; a change that cannot satisfy a precondition does not proceed and escalates.
- **Deprecation is announced, time-bounded, and reversible.** A capability, contract, or schema is retired only along a managed path that honors its guarantees until an announced, finite window elapses; the window is never compressed to a silent break.
- **A migration preserves integrity throughout and is reversible.** A migration holds validity, consistency, referential integrity, and tenant isolation along an all-or-nothing, reversible path owned by `data-model-and-entity-spec.md`; a failed or reverted evolution recovers along the fallback ladder and the rollback other documents own.
- **A dependency change is re-evaluated for its license.** A change to a third-party dependency re-triggers the license check of `legal-and-licensing-constraints.md`; a prohibited or unconfirmed license is refused, never carried through a change window.
- **Every change is accompanied by the documentation update it requires, verified for cross-document consistency.** A change and the update to the governing documents it alters are one unit; the library never drifts from the platform, ownership remains singular, and the corpus remains internally non-contradictory. The documentation updates are verified consistent across the library — fully propagated, every enumeration and cross-reference still agreeing — before the change is complete.
- **Changes requiring human review are explicit and honored.** A backward-incompatible change, a deprecation or migration, a change to a fixed architectural decision or a governing document's owned scope, a license-obligation change, and any enumerated approval-gate trigger each stop for review before adoption; review precedes adoption, authorizes only a managed in-bounds path, and never waives a guarantee.
- **A guarantee is never spent to evolve.** No invariant, floor, severity threshold, residency obligation, or prior guarantee is relaxed to make a change succeed, ship, or complete; a breach overrides any apparent gain.
- **Every change, deprecation, migration, documentation update, and review is traceable and attributable.** Each produces the record `audit-and-traceability.md` requires, including the guarantee affected, the managed path followed, and any review obtained.
- **The elaborations, numeric values, and review mechanics are owned elsewhere.** This document requires that the platform evolve without breaking a prior guarantee or accumulating drift; the contract- and data-specific elaborations, the concrete numbers, and the review mechanics are owned by the documents assigned to them.
- **The builder / built separation holds throughout.** This document binds the platform's own evolution and its governing library; the change management a builder applies to their own built artifact remains the builder's own.
- **Everything remains domain-neutral and platform-level.** No backward-compatibility obligation, deprecation rule, migration constraint, documentation requirement, or review trigger in this document encodes the characteristics of any single domain; all remain valid for the platform, in any tenant and any region.
